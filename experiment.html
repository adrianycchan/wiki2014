What we do

Our aim was to create a drug delivery agent that could target cancer cells, enter them, and detroy them. We based our project on past research on boron neutron capture therapy. If we could find some way to introduce boron 10 into cancerous cells, then theoretically we could irradiate the boron with low energy neutrons (relatively harmelss to the rest of the body) causing spontaneous nuclear decay and destructive radiation in a localized area, hopefully destroying the cancerous cell, and not too many cells around it. We coated 5 nm gold nanoparticles with alternating layers of cationic and anionic polyelectrolytes. Cationic layers included BPA-Poly(fluorescein isothiocyanate allylamine hydrochloride), our boron was contained within this compound, as well as a flourescent agent to aid with characterization. Anionic layers included Borono-Poly(styrene sulfonate) Sodium Salt (B-PSS) B-FITC-PAH) and contained additional boron.

Characterization

  • Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS) was used to characterize particle size and zeta potential. Zeta potential initially increases after the second layer, then gradually decreases
  • Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM) was used to confirm the presence of boron and support DLS results. Results show particles of expected size. the small dark dots embedded in the larger particles confirm the presence of boron. Significant particle aggregation was observed, consistent with observed zeta potential

Our Next Steps

Prior to conjugating these particles to our DNA walker (last years project), we will use AFM to characterize the walkers. It is our hope that the successful conjugation of these particles will demonstrate the particles versatility and possible usefulness as a drug delivery agent. We also hope to conjugate biotinylated streptavidin and quantum dots to the walker via "walking" and to characterize this conjugation. Following this we will begin working on a brand new project to be determined by the research team
